Everything you need to know about your machines

One plugin. Zero install on targets. If SSH works, Leassh works.

Screen Time

Know what they're doing

Automatic activity tracking via periodic screenshots analyzed by a local AI. See daily breakdowns: Gaming 2h, YouTube 3h, Homework 45min. Stacked time bars, category trends, weekly reports.

Limits

Boundaries that enforce themselves

Set daily time limits per category — 2h gaming, 3h YouTube. Leassh warns when limits approach and can automatically close games when time is up. Configurable from warn to block.

Safety

AI-powered content awareness

A local vision model analyzes screenshots for concerning content. Get immediate alerts, or a calm evening digest of the day's activity. Factual, not paranoid. You set the sensitivity.

Fleet

Monitor all your machines

CPU, GPU, RAM, disk, processes, idle time, logged-in users — probed via SSH every two minutes. Works on Windows, macOS, and Linux. Live dashboard updates in real time.

Automation

Rules that act for you

Define rules in YAML. When a shared machine goes idle, reclaim it for GPU workloads. Fire webhooks to Home Assistant or n8n. Publish to MQTT. Run commands directly via SSH.

Notifications

Stay informed, your way

Telegram alerts, webhook notifications, or OpenClaw agent integration. Get notified when machines go offline, time limits are reached, or unusual activity is detected.

Three minutes to your first dashboard

Enable SSH on your computers

Windows 10/11, macOS, and Linux all have SSH built in — it just needs to be turned on. We provide step-by-step guides for each.

Add your machines to Leassh

Enter each computer's hostname and SSH user. Leassh tests the connection, auto-detects OS, GPU, and available tools. No software installed on the machines.

Set limits and go

Configure time limits per category, enable screen time tracking, and choose your notification channel. The family dashboard shows everything at /family.

Your data never leaves your home

Every other parental monitoring tool sends your child's screen data to their cloud servers. Leassh is different. Screenshots are captured via SSH, analyzed by a vision AI running on your own hardware, and summaries stay on your network. No cloud service ever sees your child's screen. No third-party database stores their browsing history. No company profits from your family's data. Just your machines, your local AI, and your peace of mind.
