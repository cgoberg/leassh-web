Best Parental Controls for Android 2026: Complete Comparison

Ingrid Larsen April 10, 2026

If you're looking for parental controls for your child's Android device, you have fewer real options than you might think. As of 2026, several major parental control providers still don't offer Android monitoring. Net Nanny, one of the most established names in the industry, has promised Android support for years with no release date in sight.

I've tested every major parental control solution on the market: Family Link, Qustodio, Norton Family, Bark, and Leassh. Here's what actually works for monitoring Android devices in 2026.

Quick Answer: For Android-specific monitoring, your best options are Google's free Family Link (basic controls) or paid solutions like Qustodio and Leassh (more advanced features). Net Nanny cannot monitor Android at all.

1. Google Family Link — The Free Option

G
Google Family Link
Free

Family Link is Google's free parental control solution for Android. It allows parents to create Google accounts for children, approve or block app downloads, set screen time limits, and remotely lock devices. It's built into Android and works seamlessly with Google Play.

What Works Well

  • Completely free: No subscription, no hidden costs, no premium tier
  • Seamless Android integration: Works with Google Play, Chrome, YouTube Kids
  • Easy setup: Install the app and follow the wizard
  • Remote device lock: Can lock your child's phone instantly at bedtime
  • App approval workflow: Kids can't download anything without permission
  • Location tracking: See where your child's device is located

Limitations

  • Basic web filtering: Can block explicit sites, but not granular category filtering
  • Google account required: Your child needs a Google account (most do, but not all)
  • App time only: Shows minutes per app, not what they were doing inside the app
  • Can be bypassed: Tech-savvy kids can sometimes disable the app or use workarounds
  • Mobile only: Doesn't monitor what happens on desktop computers
  • All data to Google: No privacy option — everything goes to Google's servers
Best for: Families on a budget who want basic Android monitoring. It's an excellent starting point and works well for simple screen time management and app approval.

2. Qustodio — The Feature-Rich Option

Q
Qustodio
$54.95/year (basic plan)

Qustodio is one of the most comprehensive parental control solutions available. It offers advanced web filtering, detailed time limits, social media monitoring, and panic button features. The Android app provides robust monitoring capabilities.

What Works Well

  • Advanced web filtering: Granular category blocking and custom URL lists
  • Detailed reports: Shows what websites were visited, time spent per app
  • Flexible time limits: Set different schedules for weekdays vs. weekends
  • Social media monitoring: Can monitor Facebook, Instagram, Snapchat activity
  • Panic button: Kids can send an alert if they feel unsafe
  • Cross-platform: Works on Android, iOS, Windows, macOS, Fire tablets

Limitations

  • Requires app installation: Must install Qustodio app on the Android device
  • Can be circumvented: Known to be bypassed by kids who use VPNs or uninstall the app
  • Pricing: More expensive than Family Link, with tiered feature levels
  • Data to cloud: All monitoring data stored on Qustodio's servers
  • Battery drain: Continuous background monitoring can affect battery life
  • False positives: Web filtering can sometimes block legitimate educational sites
Best for: Families who need advanced features like granular web filtering and detailed activity reports. Good for parents who want more control than Family Link provides.

3. Norton Family — The Established Option

N
Norton Family
$49.99/year

Norton Family is from the cybersecurity company NortonLifeLock. It offers web supervision, time supervision, and location tracking. The solution has been around for years and integrates with Norton antivirus products.

What Works Well

  • Web supervision: Blocks inappropriate websites and allows site-by-site approval
  • Time supervision: Set daily screen time limits and scheduled downtime
  • Location tracking: Monitor your child's device location
  • Search supervision: Can monitor and block certain search terms
  • Trusted brand: Norton is a well-established cybersecurity company
  • Integration with Norton products: Works seamlessly if you use Norton antivirus

Limitations

  • Requires app installation: Must install Norton Family app
  • Less detailed than Qustodio: Fewer features compared to competitors
  • No behavioral analysis: Doesn't provide insights into what your child is actually doing
  • Cloud-only: No local privacy option
  • Android limitations: Some features work better on iOS
Best for: Families already in the Norton ecosystem who want straightforward web filtering and time management without advanced features.

4. Bark — The AI-Powered Option

B
Bark
$14/month or $79/year

Bark uses AI to monitor children's devices and messages for signs of cyberbullying, depression, suicidal ideation, and other concerning behaviors. It focuses on content monitoring rather than time limits.

What Works Well

  • AI-powered alerts: Scans texts, emails, and social media for concerning content
  • Cyberbullying detection: Can alert parents to potential bullying situations
  • Wellness focus: Designed to catch mental health concerns early
  • Multi-platform: Monitors 30+ apps and platforms including WhatsApp, Snapchat, Instagram
  • Simple setup: Connects to accounts rather than requiring app installation on all devices

Limitations

  • No time management: Doesn't offer screen time limits or app blocking
  • Privacy concerns: Scans all messages and content — significant privacy implications
  • Premium pricing: More expensive than many competitors
  • False positives: AI can sometimes flag innocent content as concerning
  • Requires account access: Needs login credentials for monitoring
  • Cloud dependency: All data processed on Bark's servers
Best for: Families concerned about cyberbullying and online safety rather than screen time management. Best as a supplement to, not replacement for, other parental controls.

5. Leassh — The Privacy-Focused Option

L
Leassh
See pricing

Leassh takes a fundamentally different approach to parental monitoring. Instead of requiring apps to be installed on target devices, Leassh uses SSH (the standard remote access protocol built into Mac and Linux) to monitor what applications are running. For Android, this means monitoring through the Linux subsystem that Android runs on.

What Works Well

  • No app installation: Uses existing SSH — nothing to install on target devices
  • Local privacy option: Monitoring data can stay on your home network
  • Process-level detail: See exactly which applications were running, at what times
  • Weekly narrative reports: Tells a story about what your child has been working on
  • Cross-platform: Works on Android, Mac, Linux, Windows (via SSH)
  • Transparent: Your child knows about the monitoring — no hidden processes

Limitations

  • Technical setup: Requires SSH access to be enabled (usually on by default on Mac)
  • Not mobile-first: Designed primarily for desktop/laptop monitoring
  • Learning curve: More technical to configure than Family Link
  • Not free: Subscription-based service
  • Android specifics: Requires SSH setup on Android (via Termux or similar)
Best for: Privacy-conscious families, mixed-OS households, and parents who want behavioral understanding rather than just time limits. Excellent for families who also need desktop monitoring.

What About Net Nanny?

Net Nanny has been a major player in parental controls for decades. They're known for excellent web filtering. But here's the critical limitation for 2026: Net Nanny still cannot monitor Android devices.

According to multiple 2026 reviews and direct communication with Net Nanny support, they have no Android child monitoring app and no specific release date. Their support team has confirmed they don't have Android support planned, despite promising it "soon" for years.

The bottom line: If your family has any Android devices that children use, Net Nanny simply isn't an option. Full stop.

Which Should You Choose?

The right choice depends on your family's specific needs:

Choose Family Link if:

  • You're on a budget (it's completely free)
  • You want basic screen time management and app approval
  • Your family uses Android devices primarily
  • You don't need advanced features like granular web filtering

Choose Qustodio if:

  • You need advanced web filtering
  • You want detailed activity reports
  • Your family uses multiple platforms (Android, iOS, desktop)
  • You're willing to pay for more features

Choose Norton Family if:

  • You're already in the Norton ecosystem
  • You want straightforward web filtering and time management
  • You don't need advanced features

Choose Bark if:

  • You're primarily concerned about cyberbullying and online safety
  • You want AI-powered content monitoring
  • You don't need screen time management

Choose Leassh if:

  • You value privacy and want local data options
  • Your family uses mixed platforms (Android + Mac/Linux/Windows)
  • You want behavioral understanding, not just time limits
  • You're comfortable with more technical setup
  • You need desktop monitoring (which Family Link can't provide)

The Honest Truth

Family Link is an excellent free option for Android-specific monitoring. It covers the basics well: app approval, screen time limits, and remote device locking. For families who only need these features, it's hard to beat.

Qustodio and Norton Family are solid paid alternatives if you need more advanced features. They're both established solutions with good track records.

Bark takes a different approach focused on content monitoring rather than time limits. It's useful for specific concerns about cyberbullying but doesn't replace traditional parental controls.

Leassh offers something different: a privacy-focused approach that works across all platforms without requiring app installation. It's particularly useful for families who need desktop monitoring in addition to Android support.

The key question is: what do you actually need? If you only need Android monitoring, Family Link is an excellent starting point. If you also need desktop monitoring, or if you value privacy and local data, Leassh might be the better complement.
